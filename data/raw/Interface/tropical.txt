Terrain descriptions
	
Tropical

*Tees
Warm ocean breezes and an open, level hitting area make this the "only" place to be when starting a hole.  
*Green
Watered, fed, and maintained to exacting standards; welcomes well-placed approach shots (press <tab> for tricky green).        
*Sand Trap
Moderately difficult hazard that tests players' course management abilities  (press <tab> to rotate).
*Rough
Dense, shaggy grass (2-3" in height) inhibits roll and deadens bounce.  Tough to make solid contact out of this cabbage.
*Pot Bunker
An open sand pit where poorly-hit shots go to die.  Players need divine intervention from the golf gods to get back into play. 
*Stream
Easier to climb every mountain than to try and ford this stream.  Scores take a bath along with the ball; take one penalty stroke.       
*Water
The Bermuda Triangle of hazards.  Players finding themselves at the bottom of this well may want to send out a search party.    
*Tropical Bush
Recovery skills and lots of luck required to get away from this fruity stuff (press <tab> to size).
*Tropical Tree
Players hitting into this wood best hope for a lucky bounce.  Green and tangled and unforgiving (press <tab> to size).
*Palm Tree
Golfers trying to get past these tropical delights might consider prayer as an option to a mid-iron (press <tab> to size).  
*Fairway
A closely cut and well-tended waystation between tee and green.  Shots roll long and bounce high.
*Firm Fairway
Just like your old, familiar fairway, but with more "zip".  Provides more roll, higher bounce, and tighter lie than a traditional fairway.
*Deep rough
Unkempt grasses that are merciless on shots that stray from the fairway.  Recovery shots out of deep rough tend to squirt and fly.  "FORE!"
*Waste bunker
Two hazards for the price of one: nasty brush jutting out of a coarse sand bed.  Stuff of golf nightmares.  
*Brush
Twisted and thorny low brush.  Has been known to reduce players to sobbing heaps.
*Rocks
Black lava puncuates the otherwise lush green landscape.  Extra "wince" practice for those unlucky enough to hit it here.  

*END
