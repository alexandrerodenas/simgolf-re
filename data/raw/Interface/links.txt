Terrain descriptions
	
Links

*Tees
Short grass and level ground provide the perfect lie.  All great golf holes start here.  
*Green
The ultimate destination for cooperative golf shots.  Marked with a targeting flag...just in case (press <tab> for tricky green).        
*Sand Trap
Shallow trench filled with washed, fine-grain sand challenges players’ shot-making abilities.  Best to avoid this cat box (press <tab> to rotate).
*Rough
Well-fed highland bentgrass allowed to grow just tall enough to slow down promising drives and hinder approach shots.  
*Pot Bunker
A signature hazard of Links courses, players may be tempted to use a “hand wedge” to return to play. 
*Burn
A jagged shallow stream that can be a magnet for poorly placed shots.  Adds an unwanted stroke to every player’s scorecard.       
*Water
Trolling for Nessie is more fun than searching for shots that end up here.  More evidence that golf balls do not float.    
*Maple Tree
Getting past this obstacle and back onto the fairway without bodily injury is an honorable goal (press <tab> to size).
*Pine
Hitting a solid shot out of the mess of needles and cones this tree deposits on the course is a chance event (press <tab> to size).
*Scots Pine
A sticky-armed creature that gathers errant shots and blocks any attempts to salvage par (press <tab> to size).  
*Fairway
Neatly clipped colonial bent grasses promote healthy bounces and lengthy rolls.  A natural place to rest on the way to the green.
*Firm Fairway
Short, tight grass on compacted soil.  Shots landing here travel with more urgency toward green and bunker alike.
*Deep rough
Wild, tangled grasses inflate scores and humble club members.  Tough to get out without a sickle.
*Waste bunker
A traditional bunker dotted with patches of gorse and brush.  As if a traditional sand bunker isn't hard enough...  
*Gorse
Little yellow blooms belie its evil intent; takes balls and keeps 'em.
*Rocks
Natural formations produce unnatural bounces.  Leaves scars on balls, clubs, and players' psyches.
