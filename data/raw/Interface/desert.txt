Terrain descriptions

Desert

*Tees
The best place to start a hole, whether building or playing.  Gourmet grasses on a level, packed-earth foundation.  
*Green
Highest quality bentgrass cradles approach shots and provides a fair test for putters (press <tab> for tricky green).        
*Sand Trap
Sculpted trenches filled with clean, raked sand make for a moderately deadly hazard.  Weekend duffers beware (press <tab> to rotate).
*Desert
Who needs hazards when the course is surrounded by desert?  Stops drives short and makes second shots less predictable. 
*Pot Bunker
As hazards go, this is Sand Trap's evil twin.  Deeper trench, heavier sand, harder to get out safely.  Yuck. 
*Ravine
A waterless stream where balls roll in and stay for awhile.  Players lose a stroke without hitting a shot.       
*Water
This would-be oasis turns out to be a mirage that gobbles up poor shots.  Penalty stroke is definitely not an illusion.    
*Joshua Tree
Arms outstretched, this hazard beckons players to a better place.  Shoulda stayed in the fairway (press <tab> to size).
*Cactus
Easier to give this porcupine a big hug than to try and hit past it (press <tab> to size).
*Palm Tree
Thick-trunked umbrellas provide needed shade for players just hacking through (press <tab> to size).  
*Fairway
Cross-cut and bright green target for well-placed drives.  Usually provides a perfect lie for approach shots.
*Firm Fairway
More freeway than fairway, shots hit here get an extra-high bounce and keep on truckin'.
*Rough
Thick green spaghetti usually seen lining fairways, waiting to grab shots that stray off-line.  At least it's green.   
*Waste bunker
Ugly brush growing wildly out of a coarse sand bunker gives the players something extra to curse.  
*Brush
Dry, half-dead tangle of weed.  Impossible lie.  Drive killer.  Makes players consider bowling instead.  
*Rocks
Players witness the dreaded pinball effect if shots land here.  Evil lie and unpredictable, if entertaining, bounce.  

*END
