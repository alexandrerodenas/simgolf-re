Terrain descriptions
	
Parklands

*Tees
Flat, green, and well tended.  Perfect for launching a drive down the middle of the fairway…or into obscurity.  
*Green
Where approach shots stick and putts roll fair and true.  Grade AAA Bermuda grass, neatly manicured (press <tab> for tricky green).        
*Sand Trap
Dry, small-grain silica fill this hazard, making for a fair test of players’ recovery skills.  Not your typical day at the beach (press <tab> to rotate).
*Rough
Thick, long, tough grass that swallows up golf balls and makes players hate their hats.  Second shots doubly challenging if a drive ends up here.
*Pot Bunker
Heavy, coarse sand lines the bottom of this pit.  Players may as well dial 911 if a shot lands here. 
*Stream
A gentle rush of cool water that will have players fishing for errant shots.  One stroke penalty assured.      
*Water
Clear, blue, and unforgiving.  The most intimidating of all hazards, place these judiciously or watch your membership sail away.  
*Tree
Mature, leafy, and formidable obstacle between your players and par.  It’s best to play it safe from this lie (press <tab> to size).
*Pine tree
Makes an unfettered shot at the green or fairway virtually impossible.  Players don’t want to hike in this forest (press <tab> to size).
*Palm tree
Palm tree on a Parklands course?  It's a bonus feature! (press <tab> to size).  
*Fairway
Close-cropped, high-quality turf that welcomes well-placed shots.  Drives that find the “short grass” bounce high and roll long.
*Firm Fairway
Watered less frequently and cut shorter than its Fairway cousin.  Shots bounce higher and roll further on this designer carpet.
*Deep rough
Long, wild, untended grass makes golf balls disappear and players curse your good name.
*Waste bunker
Fill a wide trench with coarse sand and lay in some brush and natural vegetation.  Watch your players beg for mercy.
*Brush
Low, tangled, undergrowth.  Players hitting into this stuff may as well pack a lunch...they're not coming out for a while.
*Rocks
Harsh outcroppings make for unpredictable bounce and aborted roll.  Will lead to the ruination of clubs, spikes, and SimGolf rounds.
*END
